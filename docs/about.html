About FlatFree

Our mission, philosophy, and values

What is FlatFree?

FlatFree is a community-maintained Flatpak repository built on the belief that open source software should welcome everyone. We host Flatpak applications that respect user freedom — accepting submissions based on objective, automated criteria rather than subjective human review.

Founded as an alternative to repositories that impose gatekeeping policies, FlatFree exists to ensure that free software remains truly open: open to all developers, all tools, and all methods of creation. If your software is free, builds correctly, and passes a malware scan, it belongs here — no matter who you are or how you built it.

Our repository is fully compatible with the Flatpak ecosystem. You can add FlatFree as a remote and install applications just like you would from any other Flatpak repository.

Our Philosophy

FlatFree operates on a simple principle: no gatekeeping. We believe that the open source community should be defined by what software does, not by how it was made or who made it.

We accept all free and open source software that meets three objective criteria: an OSI-approved license, a valid flatpak-builder manifest that builds successfully, and a clean malware scan. That's it. No review board. No subjective quality standards. No moral policing. No questions about whether AI was used, whether the app is "polished enough," or whether it duplicates existing functionality.

This philosophy extends to how we run the repository itself. Our infrastructure is transparent, our processes are automated, and our criteria are public. Every submission follows the same rules — applied consistently by machines, not by human judgment.

We believe that lowering the barrier to entry strengthens the entire open source ecosystem. Every developer who contributes to FlatFree is one more voice in the free software community. Every app we host is one more reason for users to choose free software.

Why Not Flathub?

Flathub is the largest Flatpak repository, and for many users it works well. But in 2026, Flathub adopted submission guidelines that fundamentally changed what it means to host software there. Specifically, they began rejecting applications "generated predominantly by AI with minimal human involvement" — a policy that introduces subjective judgment, punishes transparency, and excludes developers based on their tools rather than their results.

FlatFree differs from Flathub in several key ways:

  • No subjective review. Flathub uses human maintainers to review submissions. FlatFree uses automated CI checks. The computer decides, consistently and without bias.
  • No tool policing. Flathub rejects AI-assisted submissions. FlatFree welcomes them. We believe any tool used by a human is human work.
  • No gatekeeping. Flathub reserves the right to reject submissions based on undefined "quality standards." FlatFree has three objective, published criteria that apply to everyone equally.
  • No moral audits. Flathub's policies create a system where developers are judged on subjective measures of "human involvement." FlatFree does not ask how your code was written.

We are not here to replace Flathub or to compete with it. We are here to provide an alternative for developers and users who believe that open source should mean truly open — open to everyone, open to all tools, and open to innovation in how software is made.

Our Commitment

FlatFree is built on three core commitments that guide everything we do:

Transparency

Our submission criteria, our infrastructure, and our processes are fully public. There are no hidden rules, no secret review guidelines, no internal appeals process — because there is nothing to appeal. If your submission passes the automated checks, it is accepted. If it fails, the CI tells you exactly why. Every step is documented and reproducible.

Accessibility

We believe that contributing to open source should be easy. Our submission process is designed to be as simple as possible: fork the repository, add your manifest, open a pull request. No joining a mailing list, no waiting for approval, no navigating opaque review processes. If you can write a flatpak-builder manifest, you can submit to FlatFree.

Freedom

The four essential freedoms of open source software — to use, study, share, and modify — apply regardless of how the software was created. We do not add additional requirements that restrict these freedoms. Your right to build, distribute, and maintain free software should not depend on the approval of a review board or on adherence to subjective cultural norms about what constitutes "real" development work.

These commitments are not aspirational — they are operational. Every decision we make, every tool we build, and every policy we adopt is measured against them. FlatFree exists to serve the free software community, and we are accountable to that community through openness, not authority.

FlatFree is not affiliated with Flathub or the Flatpak project.
