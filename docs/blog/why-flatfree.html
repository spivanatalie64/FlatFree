Why FlatFree Exists: An Alternative to Flathub

Back to Blog · June 8, 2026 by Natalie

The open source ecosystem has a new gatekeeper problem, and its name is Flathub.

In early 2026, Flathub — the dominant Flatpak repository — updated its submission guidelines to include a policy that bans applications "generated predominantly by AI with minimal human involvement." On the surface, this might sound reasonable. But when you dig into what it means and how it would be enforced, it reveals a deeply problematic philosophy.

The AI Policy Problem

Flathub's exact wording states:

"Applications that are generated predominantly by AI with minimal human involvement are not accepted."
— Flathub Submission Guidelines

This policy is problematic on multiple levels:

  • It's fundamentally unenforceable. There is no objective way to determine what percentage of a codebase was written by AI versus a human. Tools like GitHub's Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, and local LLMs are deeply integrated into modern development workflows. Code passes through dozens of transformations — suggestion, completion, review, refactoring — before it reaches a pull request. Tracing the "origin" of each line is impossible.
  • It punishes transparency. A developer who openly credits their use of AI tools is far more likely to be rejected than one who simply doesn't mention it. The policy incentivizes dishonesty while penalizing honesty. This is the opposite of what open source communities should encourage.
  • It excludes a new generation of developers. AI coding tools have dramatically lowered the barrier to entry for software development. People who could never have written a line of code before are now building real, useful, functional applications. Flathub's rule tells these newcomers that their work isn't welcome purely because of how it was made, not what it does.
  • It misunderstands free software. The four essential freedoms — to use, study, modify, and share — make no mention of how software is created. They are about users' rights, not about developers' methods. Software that grants these freedoms is free software, regardless of whether a human, an AI, or a collaboration between the two produced it.

Why This Matters

Flathub has become the default distribution channel for Flatpak applications. When the default platform starts excluding legitimate free software based on subjective, unenforceable criteria, it creates a systemic problem for the entire ecosystem. Developers who are rejected have no alternative — until now.

This isn't about AI-generated code. This is about who gets to decide what counts as "real" free software. Flathub has appointed themselves as arbiters of legitimate development methodology, and that's a power no centralized platform should have.

Our Philosophy

FlatFree is built on a different set of principles:

  • All free software is welcome. If your software is licensed under an OSI-approved free software license and it builds, we will distribute it. Full stop. No exceptions.
  • Objective checks, not subjective reviews. Our CI validates manifests, builds applications, and runs malware scans. These are automated, objective, and consistent. No reviewer has the power to reject your work because of a personal bias about how you made it.
  • AI assistance is not a disqualifier. We view AI as a tool — no different from a compiler, a debugger, a linter, or a code editor. Tools don't diminish human creativity; they amplify it. We actively welcome developers who are open about their use of AI.
  • Trust the community. We believe that users are capable of deciding what software is valuable to them. Our role is not to curate or judge, but to distribute. The community decides what matters.

The Bigger Picture

The debate around AI-generated code is not going away. As AI tools become more powerful and more ubiquitous, the lines between human-written and AI-generated code will blur further. Repositories that try to draw arbitrary lines today will face increasingly impossible enforcement challenges tomorrow.

FlatFree takes the long view. Rather than trying to police development methodology, we focus on what actually matters: is the software free? Does it build? Is it safe? If the answer to these questions is yes, it belongs here.

We're not trying to replace Flathub. We're providing an alternative — a repository that lives up to the true ideals of open source, where the only criterion for inclusion is that the software is free.

If that resonates with you, get involved. Submit your application, spread the word, and help us build the open repository that open source deserves.
